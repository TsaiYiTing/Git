Introduction¶

  • Smoking by pregnant women may result in fetal injury, premature birth, and low birth weight.
  • Compare the birth weights of babies born to smokers and nonsmokers.
  • Child Health and Development Studies (CHDS)a comprehensive investigation of all pregnancies that occurred between 1960 and 1967 among women in the Kaiser Foundation Health Plan in the San FranciscoEast Bay area (Yerushalmy [Yer71]).
  • Birth weight is a measure of the babys maturity.
  • Another measure of maturity is the babys gestational age, or the time spent in the womb.

The Data¶

  • The database includes all pregnancies that occurred between 1960 and 1967 among women in the Kaiser Foundation Health Plan in Oakland, California.
  • A subset of this informa-tion collected for 1236 babies those baby boys born during one year of the study who lived at least 28 days and who were single births.
  • The information available for each baby is birth weight and whether or not the mother smoked during her pregnancy.

Numerical Summaries¶

  • A measure of location is a statistic that represents the center of the data distribution.
  • One such measure is the mean # $$\bar{x}=\displaystyle\frac{1}{n}\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}x_i$$
  • One such measure is the standard deviation (SD). # $$SD(x)=\displaystyle\sqrt{\frac{1}{n}\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}(x_{i}-\bar{x})^2}$$
  • An alternative measure of location is the median.

The Normal Curve¶

  • The standard normal curve (Figure 1.7), known as the bell curve
  • It is unimodal and symmetric around 0.
  • It also follows the 68-95-99.7 rule.

FIGURE 1.7. The standard normal curve.¶

These areas and others are determined from the following analytic expression for the curve :

$$\displaystyle\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-x^2/2}$$¶

Traditionally, $\phi{(z)}$ represents the area under the normal curve to the left of $z$ :

$$\phi{(z)}=\displaystyle\int_{-\infty}^{z}\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-x^2/2}dx$$¶

Note that to standardize the data :

$$\displaystyle\frac{x_{i}-\bar{x}}{SD(x)}$$¶

Checks for normality that are more formal than the 68-95-99.7 rule are based on the coefcients of skewness and kurtosis.

$$skewness=\displaystyle\frac{1}{n}\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}\left(\frac{x_{i}-\bar{x}}{SD(x)}\right)^3$$¶

$$kurtosis=\displaystyle\frac{1}{n}\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}\left(\frac{x_{i}-\bar{x}}{SD(x)}\right)^4$$¶
